# NV0987 Lab

Experimental research environment focused on the study of color as a generative phenomenon in human perception and artificial intelligence systems.

---

## Overview

NV0987 Lab is an independent research project investigating how color emerges, evolves, and functions as a structural principle in visual systems.

The research is based on the theoretical work of Sonia Delaunay and extends these concepts into the context of AI-generated imagery. The central objective is to understand how color operates differently in human perception and in algorithmic image production.

Rather than treating color as a static attribute, the project approaches it as a dynamic, relational process shaped by time, perception, and interaction.

---

## Research Focus

The lab explores the following core questions:

- How does color emerge as a relational phenomenon in human perception?
- How does color develop through experience and time?
- How does color appear in AI-generated image systems?
- What differences exist between human and machine-based color formation?
- How do these differences affect perception, memory, and visual thinking?

The research is grounded in two primary documents:

### 1. Color as a Generative Principle in the Work of Sonia Delaunay  
**Version 9.1 – Theoretical and Historical Expansion**

This document establishes the core theoretical framework of the project. It examines color as a generative force in visual perception and connects this understanding to algorithmic image formation and artificial intelligence systems.

### 2. Interdisciplinary Foundation Edition  
**Version 2 – Conceptual and Terminological Framework**

This document expands the theoretical base into an interdisciplinary structure, introducing a consistent conceptual system for analyzing color in relation to perception, cognition, and AI-based visual processes.
