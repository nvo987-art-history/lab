Research Overview

NV0987 Lab is an independent research node focused on the study of color as a relational and generative system. The research investigates how color operates beyond static representation, emerging through interaction, perception, and structural dependency.

The work is informed by the approach of Sonia Delaunay, particularly her understanding of color as a constructive force capable of generating form. This perspective is examined in contrast with algorithmic systems, where color is typically treated as a controllable parameter.

Theoretical Position

The central premise of this research is that color cannot be fully reduced to independent variables or discrete values. Instead, it must be understood as a dynamic system defined by relations, transformations, and perceptual context.

Within this framework, color is not applied to form, but actively produces it. This establishes a fundamental distinction between painterly color systems and computational representations.

Research Domains

  • Relational color systems and interdependent structures
  • Generative color behavior in composition and spatial organization
  • Limits of algorithmic and parametric color modeling
  • Perceptual and temporal dynamics of color interaction

Current Work

The current phase of the research is based on an initial theoretical document that defines the conceptual boundaries of the project. This document functions as a foundation for further analytical and experimental development.

Ongoing work includes the refinement of core concepts, comparative analysis between painterly and algorithmic systems, and the development of experimental approaches.

Experiments

Experimental work explores how relational color systems can be observed, constructed, and tested through both visual and structural approaches.

These experiments also investigate the limitations of algorithmic models in representing generative color behavior. Results will be published progressively as the research develops.
