Research

This research examines color as a generative, relational, and temporally unfolding phenomenon, with particular attention to how this behavior appears in human perception and how it is transformed within AI-generated image environments.

The study focuses on perceptual development, especially in early cognition, where color does not appear as a fixed visual attribute but emerges through experience. It investigates how color is connected to memory, temporality, and spatial perception, and how these relationships structure visual thinking.

The work addresses the difference between naturally evolving color phenomena and AI-generated images, where color appears as an immediate outcome of a computational process. The goal is not to evaluate these approaches, but to understand how they operate through fundamentally different logics and how they produce distinct perceptual and cognitive structures.

The research also extends to pedagogical environments and visual culture, where color functions not as a decorative element but as a structuring factor in attention, learning, and experience.

Research Questions

  • How does color emerge as a relational phenomenon in human perception?
  • What role do time and experience play in the formation of color?
  • How does color appear within AI-generated image systems?
  • What structural differences exist between these modes of color formation?
  • How do these differences affect perception, memory, and visual thinking?

Documentation
