# Code of Conduct

## NV0987 Lab – Community and Research Conduct Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

NV0987 Lab operates as an independent experimental research environment within a decentralized knowledge infrastructure.

This Code of Conduct defines the expected standards of behavior for anyone engaging with this repository, its content, or its broader network.

The goal is to maintain a space that supports:

- rigorous research
- conceptual clarity
- respectful discourse
- open and responsible knowledge exchange

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all interactions, including:

- GitHub issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- external references to this repository
- contributions to research materials or documentation

---

## 3. Core Principles

### 3.1 Respect

All participants are expected to engage respectfully.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment or discrimination
- personal attacks
- dismissive or hostile communication
- intentional misrepresentation of research

---

### 3.2 Intellectual Integrity

Contributors must:

- represent ideas accurately
- clearly distinguish speculation from established claims
- avoid plagiarism
- properly reference sources when applicable

---

### 3.3 Research Responsibility

This repository contains experimental and evolving research.

Participants should:

- avoid presenting unfinished work as definitive conclusions
- acknowledge uncertainty where it exists
- engage critically, but constructively

---

### 3.4 Decentralized Identity Awareness

This project is connected to a decentralized identity system (DID).

Participants must not:

- impersonate identities
- misuse identity-linked references
- create misleading associations with the NV0987 network

---

## 4. Contributions

Contributions are welcome, but must align with:

- the conceptual scope of the project
- the structural integrity of the system
- machine-readable and semantic consistency where applicable

The maintainer reserves the right to reject contributions that:

- conflict with the research direction
- introduce noise or irrelevant material
- compromise clarity or coherence

---

## 5. Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may result in:

- removal of content
- rejection of contributions
- restriction from participation

Decisions are made to preserve the integrity of the research environment.

---

## 6. Reporting

If you encounter behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, you may report it via:

- nvo@nvo987.us

Reports will be handled with discretion.

---

## 7. Attribution

This Code of Conduct is informed by general open-source community standards, adapted to the specific context of:

- experimental research
- decentralized systems
- machine-readable knowledge environments

---

## 8. Final Note

NV0987 Lab is not a conventional open-source project.

It is an evolving research system.

Participation implies an understanding of this context and a willingness to engage accordingly.
